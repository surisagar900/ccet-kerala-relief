Kerala went through one of the worst deluges in this century. In August 2018, an unusually surplus rainy season inundated vast swathes of Kerala under water. It was the worst flood in 100 years.The deluge displaced more than 1.4 million people. More than 400 lives were lost. The rains and landslides devoured scores of houses, roads and even bridges. For the first time in history, all the reservoirs in the State had to be opened. Never before had it witnessed a calamity of this scale. The floods have caused a loss of Rs.40,000 Crore. Kerala is now on a path to rebuild the state after the floods. Though the floods have caused widespread damage and misery, it had failed to destroy the resolve of the people. Kerala is now attempting a rebuilding of the State.
